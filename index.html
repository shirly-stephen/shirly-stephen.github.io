Research

My research at the intersection of Spatial Information Science and GeoAI leverages advanced data science and computational approaches to enhance our understanding of complex environmental dynamics. My expertise spans semantic web technologies, spatial statistics, and spatial data infrastructures, which I apply to support environmental intelligence and urban applications. While my work often positions me as a geospatial data scientist, I identify more specifically as a geospatial knowledge engineer, with a focus on knowledge representation and spatial reasoningi.

My overarching goal is to develop responsible, data-driven solutions that extract meaningful insights from large-scale environmental datasets. I am particularly focused on promoting equitable and resilient environmental outcomes by improving data interoperabilityi, and spatial prediction. By employing theory-informed approaches grounded in geography and domain-specific semantics, I aim to address critical challenges posed by our rapidly changing environment. Know, Know Where, KnowWhereGraph: A densely connected, cross-domain knowledge graph and geo-enrichment service stack for applications in environmental intelligence
Krzysztof Janowicz, Pascal Hitzler, Wenwen Li, Dean Rehberger, Mark Schildhauer, Rui Zhu, Cogan Shimizu, Colby K Fisher, Ling Cai, Gengchen Mai, Shirly Stephen and others.
AI Magazine, 2022
project page / video / github

KnowWhereGraph is a framework that integrates a knowledge graph with geo-enrichment to deliver area briefings within seconds for any location on Earth. It is designed to enhance environmental intelligence, enabling decision-makers and data scientists to quickly enrich their data with billions of interconnected, up-to-date facts at the human-environment interface, thereby improving situational awareness for informed decision-making.
